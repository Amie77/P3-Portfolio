Precedent Presentation

2018 Fall

Overview

Precedent presentation is a team project from SIAT’s graphic design course. Our team’s task was to create presentation slides about the renowned Japanese graphic designer Kenya Hara. This project was designed using Adobe Illustrator.

Research & Sketch

The first thing we did before design was researching Hara’s past works and interviews. This research was done together as a team, however, each member gets a particular topic to search on. My role in the team covers choosing the font and designing the layout. After learning Hara’s concept of emptiness in design, I decided to keep the style extremely simple. The image shows my sketches for the layout.

Design Process

Most graphics we retrieved from Hara’s work shared a light and minimalist style. This leads me to think more about how to keep the texts and graphics consistent. In other words, the slide shouldn’t be filled with texts but rather keep them simple and succinct. The images shown are examples retrieved from the final presentation.

Challenge

Managing whitespaces was the utmost challenging part of this project. During the design process, I realized that excessive whitespace can make the slide look too bleak and empty. But at the same time, I also need to keep everything simple. Therefore, after trying a few different methods, I decided to add muted and pale colours that never intend to break the tranquility conveyed in these slides.

Final Reflection

While designing the slides, I gained a better understanding of minimalist design. I learnt that keeping the design simple is never a simple job. The balance between the whitespace and texts is very crucial and can be difficult to establish. Using dots of colour to embellish the generally blank design can actually help break the bleakness. I also learnt that design is a way to solve problem. A good design does not only please the eyes but also enhance the communication of ideas. In the end, the presentation received a B in general which I was quite happy with. However, I could do better if I spent more time practicing the speech.
