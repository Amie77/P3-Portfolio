PVS Advisor App

2020 Fall

Overview

PVS Advisor App was the final concept of our team design delivered at week 12 while taking the interactive design methods course. The course was heavily team-based as all projects required collaborations. While taking the course, I was in a group of six. Each team in the class had one partner organization to work with. Based on interviews, research and iterative design processes in the past eleven weeks, we deployed our knowledge of interactive design methods to solve our participant group’s problem creatively. The interactive mock-up of the app is on Figma.

Figure 1-3. Examples of PVS Advisor's interface design

Participant Group

Our participant group was Prime Vice Studios (PVS). It is a sequential art company specializing in the development of comics, graphic novels, character design, and other sequential art assets and preparing them for intellectual-property submission.

Figure 4. Logo of PVS

My Role

My role in this project was to design the app’s user interface and prototype interactions for every interactive element. The app targets two types of users, entrepreneurial artists who are seeking advice and experienced advisors. Therefore, the final design includes two interface designs, one for users and one for advisors. I was responsible for the creation of both interfaces, together with the other two teammates.

Business Problem

At week 4, we gathered and analyzed the ethnographic/interview data from PVS in regard to PVS's status quo. According to these statements, we did a thematic analysis (see the image) to help us define our design focus. The problem that PVS encountered was their ineffective communication with clients. The major problem was that the company doesn’t have a definite description of their services and breadth of knowledge in their online presence. Rather, information about available services was concealed by personal works. As a result, potential clients could only get a vague sense of what the company does. Based on PVS’s advising service for creative artists, we aimed to create streamlined communication with clients.

Persona & User Journey

Based on our research of PVS, we created a persona and user journey map for the concept. Our initial persona is a event planner & organizer of an organization who seeks . However, we later realized we found the wrong archetype. Therefore, we changed the user persona to an entrepreneurial sequential artist who is seeking help in her business and art career.

Design Focus

The foundation of the project is to ideate a viable design focus. Essentially, a design focus defines a solution that stems from the problem we wanted to solve for PVS. Our design focus was to provide entrepreneurial artists in the sequential art industry with tailored advice on monetizing their artistic skills by reducing confusion about managing and promoting their creative business and breaking the process down into clear steps tested by successful business owners.

UI/UX Design Process

When I was designing the interface for both the user’s and advisor’s side, I imagined what our persona Jennifer and the owner of PVS would expect to see on each page. To facilitate better communication, buttons and other interactive elements should seamlessly guide the user to the goal. Therefore, I tried to design the interface In other words, limiting the functions and buttons and clearer instructions can lead to less confusion. Hierarchy is an important aspect we considered during the design process. Bolded titles and colors are only applied to texts needed to be highlighted, leaving the screen a clean and straightforward interface. After the design is done, I added interactions to the prototype model so that I could personally experiment the user journey in Figma and find issues with the interaction design.

Feedback & Final Reflection

Our participant group feedback has been overwhelmingly positive. The owner of PVS believes that this solution aligns well with the company values and will help him reach out to other artists and be beneficial to both PVS and app users. Through this project, I realized that design is not only a study of visual aesthetics but also a tool for problem solving. I also realized the significance of creating personas and user journeys. Without a definite understanding of the users, UI/UX designers will design haphazardly. Learnt from this experience, I acquired a clearer understanding of the UI/UX design process and realized that every step in the process is necessary.
