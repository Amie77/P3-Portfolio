Advertisement Layout

2018 Fall

Overview

This individual project was to design an 11x17 advertising poster for an exhibition of Tadao Ando, a preeminent Japanese architect, at Vancouver Art Gallery. The objective of the project was to practice designing with a different set of constraints including colours and content while taking into account the audience. .

Inspirations from Tadao Ando

Tadao Ando is a Japanese self-taught architect who won the Pritzker Prize in 1995. As most of his work are concrete buildings, the simplicity and paleness leave a strong industrial impression. However, when Ando let elements of light and nature to leak in, a perfect fusion of nature, simplicity and modernity is presented.

Sketches

I started off by collecting some high-quality images and sketching 20 possible layouts. Among the sketches, I chose 2 options to be the draft posters.

First Draft & Problem

The first two drafts turned out to digress far away from Ando’s works. Since concrete and solemn buildings are very common in Ando’s works so it is not very appropriate to use playful colours and typography for the poster. When I was doing the first two drafts, the major problem was that I didn’t have an intended audience in mind and this led to a mistake in typography. Thus, after learning from the failure in the first draft led to my redesign of the project later.

Redesign

Taking into account the mistakes made in the first draft, I designed another two posters for the exhibition of Tadao Ando. Redesigning was the greatest challenge for me, since I had to start over again and regenerate ideas. Before I started to redesign, I did more research on Tadao Ando’s past works and interviews. I found that Ando always combines concrete buildings with light and shadow in his designs to reveal a sense of Zen and solemnity. Based on this research, I began to focus more on how I can convey the same sense in my poster. The image I chose already has an innate solemnity or peacefulness, so choosing the right typography was the major task for the poster redesign. Instead of using a serif or display fonts, I found sans serif worked much better as they are more modern and clean. Therefore in the new draft, I used sans serifs for all texts but applied different weights in each cluster in order to achieve hierarchy.

Final Poster

After getting feedback on the redesigned posters, I selected one of them as my final poster. I also made several changes to it to refine the design. In the final poster, each word in the title “Light in the Void” was rearranged to create a more interesting layout and texts are mostly aligned with shadows and the tree or aligned with one another.

Reflection

In the process of designing this project, I encountered many difficulties. The reasons for these problems stem from my lack of understanding of Tadao Ando and my audience groups. When I was redesigning the drafts, I put more effort in the preparation stage of design. That is, researching about the values which Tadao Ando has in architecture design. By doing so, I later noticed that this extra research helps me capture Ando’s design in my own design. In the final submission, I should emphasize the designer’s surname “Ando” instead of making the text “Tadao” stand out since Tadao Ando is more colloquially known as Ando.
